The Sweet Spot: 7-14 Days

Microgreens are harvested at the perfect moment - when the plant has developed its first set of true leaves but before it matures. At this stage, the plant contains the highest concentration of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants as it channels all its energy into growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about microgreens

Are microgreens safe to eat raw?

Yes, microgreens are completely safe to eat raw when grown in clean, controlled environments like ours. They're actually best eaten raw to preserve their maximum nutritional value and crisp texture.

How long do microgreens stay fresh?

When stored properly in the refrigerator, fresh microgreens typically last 5-7 days. Keep them in their original packaging or a breathable container, and avoid washing until ready to eat.

Do microgreens taste different from regular greens?

Microgreens often have more concentrated, intense flavors than their mature counterparts. For example, radish microgreens have a peppery kick, while pea shoots are sweet and tender. The flavors are generally milder and more approachable than you might expect.

Can kids eat microgreens?

Absolutely! Microgreens are excellent for children as they're nutrient-dense and often milder in taste than mature vegetables. Start with sweeter varieties like pea shoots or sunflower microgreens, and use them as fun, colorful additions to familiar foods.

How much should I eat daily?

A small handful (about 1-2 ounces) of microgreens per day can provide significant nutritional benefits. Because they're so nutrient-dense, you don't need large quantities to get the health benefits. Start small and gradually increase as you get used to incorporating them into your diet.
